Why Delta Chi

More than letters.
A standard you can live by.

Six reasons men choose Delta Chi at Purdue.

i.

A network that compounds

Delta Chi has chartered chapters at more than 130 campuses. Add Purdue's 600,000-strong alumni network on top, and your professional graph expands the moment you take the pledge pin.

ii.

Leadership you'll actually use

Run a chapter committee. Manage a budget. Plan an event. Recruit a class. These are skills employers list on job descriptions — and you'll graduate with the receipts.

iii.

A home at 501 N. Russell

Our chapter house has stood on Russell Street since 1927. Twenty thousand square feet of space — to study, to host, to call home for four years.

iv.

You build the chapter

Reestablished in Fall 2024, this chapter is being built right now. You're not inheriting traditions — you're authoring them. That's a once-in-a-generation opportunity.

v.

Service with intent

Detrash the Wabash, the V Foundation, and local Lafayette nonprofits get our time and attention. We work where it matters and we show up consistently.

vi.

Brothers, not bystanders

Formals, retreats, tailgates, late-night study sessions — and the rare kind of friendship where the relationships outlast the four years on campus.

The Process

How recruitment works.

Rush is a series of honest conversations. No tricks, no pressure — just two parties getting to know each other.

  1. i
    Step One

    Meet the brothers

    Coffee, lunch, an open event. Low-pressure conversations with actives so you can get a feel for who we are and how we operate.

  2. ii
    Step Two

    House tours & events

    Visit 501 N. Russell. Spend time at brotherhood events, philanthropy nights, or game-day tailgates. See the chapter in motion.

  3. iii
    Step Three

    Interviews

    A genuine conversation about your goals, your interests, and what you're looking for in a fraternity — and whether the chapter is the right fit for both sides.

  4. iv
    Step Four

    Bid day

    If we extend a bid and you accept, you're officially a new member of Delta Chi at Purdue.

  5. v
    Step Five

    New member education

    An eight-week program covering our history, principles, and chapter operations. You'll meet your pledge class — and form the friendships you'll carry for the rest of your life.

Who We Look For

No prototype.
One standard.

We aren't recruiting one kind of guy. Engineers and athletes, musicians and management majors, first-gen students and legacies — Delta Chi at Purdue is intentionally broad. What we do require is the same in every brother:

  • i. Character. You do the right thing when no one is watching. That is the entry fee.
  • ii. Effort. You take your academics seriously, you show up, and you carry weight in the rooms you walk into.
  • iii. Respect. For each other, for women, for the people who keep our house running, for the chapter's reputation.
  • iv. Curiosity. You're interested in something. We want brothers who care about their craft — whatever that craft is.
