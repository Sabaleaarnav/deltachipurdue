Who We Are

A new chapter in a
ninety-eight year story.

Delta Chi was founded at Cornell in 1890. The Purdue chapter was chartered in 1927 — and in the fall of 2024, a new generation of brothers brought it home. We are rebuilding a chapter that earns its letters every day: in the classroom, in service, and in how we treat each other.

We aren't a legacy waiting to be inherited. We're a chapter being built — by brothers who choose to lead, study, serve, and stand for something. If that sounds like the kind of organization you'd want to belong to, you'll find a seat at our table.

Why Delta Chi
Our Cardinal Principles

Four ideals that have
guided us since 1890.

Every brother of Delta Chi commits to four cardinal principles. They are not slogans — they are the way we judge our own conduct.

I.

Promote Friendship

We bring together men of good character and shared standards, and we keep those bonds long after graduation.

II.

Develop Character

Integrity is the entry fee. We hold each other to standards of honesty, accountability, and respect.

III.

Advance Justice

We stand up for what is right, even when it costs us. Justice is the principle that shaped our founding.

IV.

Sound Education

Scholarship comes first. Brothers support one another academically — and the chapter's standards reflect it.

Brotherhood · Leadership · Service

What we actually do.

Three things define a Delta Chi chapter, and we measure ourselves by all three.

Brotherhood
Retreats, formals, intramurals, late-night study sessions — and the kind of relationships that show up at your wedding twenty years later.
Leadership
Every brother runs something — a committee, a budget line, a project. We graduate men who already know how to lead a team.
Service
From Detrash the Wabash to philanthropy supporting the V Foundation, we put time and effort back into Lafayette and the causes that matter.
